    SYMPTOMS

    People with COVID-19 have had a wide range of symptoms reported – ranging from mild symptoms to severe illness. Symptoms may appear 2-14 days after exposure to the virus. Anyone can have mild to severe symptoms. People with these symptoms may have COVID-19:

    FEVER AND CHILLS

    If you have a high fever, you can take a fever reducer, such as acetaminophen, to help bring it down.

    ACHES AND COUGH

    If you have body aches, a sore throat or cough, a pain reliever can help lessen the discomfort these symptoms can bring..

    SHORTNESS OF BREATH
      1.Check the person's airway
      2.Loosen any tight clothing.
      3.Help the person use any prescribed medicine
      4.Bronchodilators to open airways
    LOSS OF TASTE AND SMELL
      Anosmia. Loss of sense of smell.
      Ageusia. Loss of sense of taste.
      Hyposmia. Reduced ability to smell.
    DIARRHEA

    Drink plenty of liquids, including water, broths and juices. Avoid caffeine and alcohol.

    SORE THROAT

    Gargle with a mixture of warm water and 1/2 to 1 teaspoon of salt.Cool your throat by eating a cold treat like a popsicle or ice cream.
